# Patronage

Cozy Renaissance-Italy city builder. Full design spec: @docs/design-doc.md — read it before implementing any game system (resources, commissions, buildings, worker allocation, artists, etc.).

## Non-negotiable design constraints

From the doc's "Key Design Principles" — flag it if a change would violate one of these rather than just implementing it:

- No citizen pathfinding, no granular supply chains — service/material buildings are capacity/threshold checks, not simulations. Materials are supplier capacity, never a stockpile.
- All art is commissioned (once Phase 8 lands): every artwork has a requester, a name, and stakes. Requesters (Church, noble families) are flavor on commissions — no relationship meters or neglect penalties.
- Exactly 3 headline resources (Florins, Inspiration, Prestige) + Population as status. Don't add a new top-bar resource without a real player decision behind it.
- Every building either unlocks something or passively boosts something — never requires active management.
- Spatial effects are soft: plaza proximity is a flat bonus, never a hard in/out radius.
- Renaissance = prestige milestone soft ending; play continues afterward.

- After implementing a phase in the design doc, move it to the done section. When other features are complete, mark them as *(built)*. 
- Commit messages are one line. Add a body only when the change genuinely can't be understood without one — the docs carry the reasoning, not the git log.

## Current state vs. doc phases

Phases 0–11 done (placement, tick loop, workers, artists + ranks, artworks, supplier capacity gating, commissions, work display, plaza connectivity, artist training) plus graphics G1–G4 and most of G5 (seeded water layer — five map archetypes from dry to coastal — + stone bridges; see the doc's water pass). Decorative citizens scale with population (exact when low/countable, damped toward a cap beyond — `crowdSize` in `app/game/crowd.ts`) and render as thin-instance batches (`createThinInstanceFigureFactory` in `app/game/render/citizenFigures.ts`, 15 draw calls for any crowd size); count is their only sim coupling — still cosmetic random-walkers, no pathfinding. Phase 9 work display: typed display slots on buildings/plazas (`app/game/display.ts`), marble statues on plinths + framed paintings on easels (`app/game/render/displayArt.ts`), displayed works trickle inspiration/prestige + boost their host, click-a-building `DisplayPanel` + Gallery "Display at…". Phase 11 training: continuous XP (`app/game/artists.ts` `progressArtworks` + `XP_RATES`) — passive practice every month for artists in an active workshop, multiplied when a higher-ranked workshop-mate teaches, plus the completion bonus. Next up: Phase 12 Renaissance milestone. Roads and houses are player-placed individually — that's intentional current scope (zoning is a later phase; auto-roads were cut); all five road variants drag at 45° diagonals — paved roads plus dirt path and stone bridge (diagonal crossings get an unrotated junction plate; see the doc's Diagonal streets). Buildings rotate in 45° steps (R cycles 8; `Tile.rotation` 4–7 = quarter + 45°, claiming a diamond cell mask via `footprintMask` in `app/game/buildings.ts` — never treat a diagonal footprint as its bounding box), and holding Shift while placing snaps the ghost flush to a nearby road, auto-facing it (`app/game/roadSnap.ts`); row-house blending and colonnade extend are deliberately skipped at 45° (see the doc's Snap-to-road section). A main menu fronts the boot (`app/game/ui/MainMenu.tsx`: continue, new game with optional seed or archetype pick via `seedForArchetype`, demo tour — `?demo` is no longer dev-only); Settings → Main Menu reloads back to it, and the save only hydrates on Continue. Numbers pass (July 2026): XP runs on a ×100 scale (`RANK_XP` 400–4000, all `XP_RATES` gains integers; save v7 migrates old artists ×100) and the roster shows founder progress ("640 / 900 XP", `nextRankXp` in `app/game/artists.ts`); the first building-effects wave is wired — cottage/townhouse rents (2ƒ/5ƒ), chapel passive amenity (+10, workerless), palazzo `housing: 12`, cathedral consecration lump (`prestigeOnBuild: 25`, paid in `placeTiles`), decoration inspiration trickles (per-cell rates on linear pieces), and the bell tower as a secondary hub — `connectivity.ts` now derives `PLAZA_IDS` from the `isHub` metadata flag instead of hard-coding plaza ids. Requester-pool shaping (cathedral elevation, palazzo noble installs) still waits on the factions phase. Late-game money rebalance (July 2026): florins stay the constraint resource while prestige keeps the full reward curve — house rent scales with occupancy (`min(1, population/housing)` in `app/game/tick.ts`, replacing flat-per-house rent), duplicate non-housing florin generators (e.g. a second Market) get geometric diminishing returns (`INCOME_DIMINISHING_RETURNS`), and commission florin rewards are compressed against artist rank (`FLORIN_RANK_COMPRESSION` in `app/game/commissions.ts`) while commission prestige is untouched. No upkeep, no new sink — the existing big-ticket build-out is the outlet, and a second wave deepens that outlet as the city grows: duplicate workshops/suppliers/services (`costEscalates` in `app/game/buildings.ts` — `artist`/`materials`/`service` types) cost progressively more (`COST_ESCALATION`, `escalatedCost`/`buildOrderRank`), priced live off the current tile map with no persisted counter or save migration; raze salvage (`getRazeSalvage`) tracks the escalated price actually paid rather than the flat base cost. Market Stall (July 2026): the first `placesOnRoads` building — a workerless 1×1 street stall placed onto plain cardinal road cells or a plaza's outer ring (non-origin perimeter cells, mask-based `isPlazaRimCell` so stalls never erode inward — plinth slots are all interior; overwrites the cell via the shared `checkCell` rule in `placementRules.ts`; blocked on bridges, diagonal ribbons, plaza interiors) and conducting plaza connectivity at road cost (`ROAD_OVERLAY_IDS` in `connectivity.ts`, derived from the same flag) so 1-wide paths never sever; its small florin/amenity output scales by the per-building `connectionBonus` metadata field (stall 1.0; everything else defaults to the global 0.25 via `connectionBonusOf`) — and, since the foot-traffic pass (July 2026), the stall's bonus is real traffic, not a flat sensitivity: `footTraffic` metadata flag → `boost = 1 + connectionBonus × hubStrength × bustle × catchment` (`app/game/traffic.ts`: `bustle` = `crowdCurve(population)/BUSTLE_FULL` — the visible crowd's curve, extracted from `crowdSize` in `crowd.ts`; `catchment` = housing capacity within `CATCHMENT_REACH` network cells / `CATCHMENT_FULL`, WeakMap-memoized by tiles identity like connectivity; knobs in `constants.ts`; both factors monotonic non-decreasing — principle 6 — with `traffic.check.ts` asserting it; threaded through `tick.ts` `plazaBoost`, `computeCityMetrics` (new trailing `population` param, 0 = muted), and the tooltip's "Foot traffic: +N%" line; all derived, no save migration). Its model is the kit stall reshaped one-sided (`scripts/make-stall-side.py` → `stall-side-*.glb`: ridge slid back, long awning slope presenting +X to the street); the Market building keeps the symmetric booths. Sandstone residences (July 2026): cottage/townhouse facades read as stone — five canvas-drawn patterns, weighted by repetition (`STONE_TINTS` in `app/game/render/wallTexture.ts`) replace the cream/pink/sand/white stucco spread in `FACADE_PALETTES.residential`, riding the existing position-hashed tint path (`getTintedPair` puts the DynamicTexture on the tint clone, so the base `stucco` material, its pinned #dcd0b6 average, and every non-residential `proc:block` ref are untouched); `proc:gable-end` gained planar UVs so the courses continue up the gable. Batch-1 fittings generated (July 2026): the artist-brief pieces built in code instead of commissioned (`proc:surround-rect`/`surround-arch`/`door-frame`/`door-leaf`/`arch-bay` in `proceduralPieces.ts` — pietra-serena boxes + 6–8-facet voussoir wedge fans, one `stone`/`wood` material each so `Part.tint` recolors whole fittings): every house window wears a real stone surround around its reveal + louvre leaf and house doors are frame + planked leaf (`windowOn`/`houseFront`), the palazzo's piano nobile got nine arched surrounds (`archWindow`), and `wall-arch.glb` retired to 0 refs (`TINT_COLORS.reveal` darkened to #453d33 so shutterless reveals survive direct sun; `proc:arch-bay` serves the palazzo loggia and colonnade — the cathedral's verde blind arcade was replaced by window rows in the marble pass below). The commission (docs/artist-brief.md) now starts at batch 2 — bifora/rose/dome/ivy, the genuinely organic pieces; see docs/procedural-pieces.md. Marble pass (July 2026): the bell tower and cathedral went panel-free onto a hand-drawn white-marble inlay texture (`campanile` in `render/wallTexture.ts` — green runs vertically as pilasters/panel edges, hairline horizontal courses, rose slivers; STONE_TINTS membership routes it, no palette) — the campanile is a rebuilt 5-storey slim shaft with `archWindow`s (now scale-parameterized) growing up to a 4-face belfry under a projecting crown, while the cathedral hangs the marble as a Santa Croce **screen facade** (thin front slabs + pediment via `gableRoof`'s new `tint` override, plus gable-end wedges capping the aisle slopes; the front wears `screen` — the campanile language densified into the SMN panel grid with alternating verde/rose lozenges) over brown rubble flanks (`flank` in wallTexture.ts; the shell is unit columns — `proc:block@1x3` nave at 2.5 tall, `@1x2` aisles at 1.75 — so courses neither stretch nor mismatch) carrying a five-bay window rhythm: arched clerestories per side plus arched-over-rectangular rows on the aisles (`windowOn` gained a `wall` param like `archWindow`'s). Portals are scaled house door fittings until a landmark portal piece exists — noted in docs/procedural-pieces.md open work.
